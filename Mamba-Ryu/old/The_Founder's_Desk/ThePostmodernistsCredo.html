The Postmodernist's Credo

I believe there is but one Truth:
That in time and space we trace a path
From birth to inevitable oblivion
And that over that dominion
No gods born to man have say          
I believe that Heaven or Hell is other people.

I believe that to live is to struggle,
To stand only to fall again,
Sometimes to yield and at others to contend,
Yet always to battle against that bitter end,
And then,
As that final moment takes its course,
To curse or to endorse a lifetime's passing.

I believe in honesty and integrity,
Yet in wisdom before fact or truth
And that much of one's youth is spent
In search of such distinctions.

I believe that humankind is cursed by immortal addictions
And that no greater folly
Created further afflictions in this world

I believe that white men can jump.
                             
I believe in family
And in the value of a true friend,
And in loyalty and in just retribution,
For in that fair distribution of acts there is no crime.

I believe that friends are those who win when you win
And share in your losses and defeats.

I believe that ‘spirit’ is the metal forged
When mind and body join to challenge the fires of adversity

I believe in character and in presence
And that the essence of a man or woman,
Can only be found as when balancing at the edge of the abyss
And through each narrow miss of steps misplaced
We establish who we really are

I believe that I must avoid before I injure,
Injure before I maim,
Maim before I kill,
But if the challenge carries me further still,
Then slay I must and will,
To save family, self or friend

I believe that the greatest transgressions
Have been committed by those armed with fictitious creeds
Uttered by confabulated beings
Responsible for fabricated deeds

I believe that love is the one reliable palliative
Against the benign indifference of the Universe

I believe that humankind’s capacity for illusion
Renders more often than not
A self-defeating delusion
That ensnares and entraps with impalpable bonds

I believe that against the vice of requesting
Is the virtue of not providing

I believe that if you run from reality or yourself,
You’ll only get tired,
But that most become mired precisely in that flight

I believe that the actions that mark one's life
Should be painted in bold and broad strokes

I believe that Faith means not wanting to know what is true

I believe that the genocide of a nation
Continues with the eradication of another’s past,
Deprecation of its present,
And the implantation of alien idols
In the image and semblance of its oppressors

I believe that animals have minds

I believe that the best a man can be
Is a father to those in need
And by deed a brother to his fellow being

I believe that patience is a virtue,
But only while waiting,
And that in hating there is no reward

I believe that vision is the best defense
Against life's insurmountable odds,
And it is in its enactment that we become
Like the very gods we have created

I believe that to know and not to do is not to know

I believe that greatness is born only of life's school of war;
And that life, or death,
Are individual choices that each and every one us have the right to bear

I believe that each of us is born a piece of metal or a rock,
Or perhaps a wooden block,
And against life's jabs and gashes,
We must add our own slashes of the chisel
And make ourselves into works of art

I believe that poverty is the only original sin

I believe in the power of the will,
And the will to power,
And that where there is a will there is most often a way,
And that the way of the will together with the skill to survive,
Will keep the dreams alive,
Dreams that shed light into the darkness of the day-mare

I believe that virtue is found
In that ineffable and sacred space
Between being and becoming

I believe in demanding perfection,
But settling for excellence

I believe that truth is a scarce resource
And that it should be employed judiciously

I believe in nurture over nature,
And that our society is guilty of neglect
When it condemns the young of mind
To the cells of institutions
Rather than training them to make their own contributions
To the whole

I believe that the purpose of education
Is to learn to separate fact from fiction
In the pursuit of the inalienable essentials of human existence

I believe that friends forget,
Family dies, and possessions lose their shine,
But only greatness knows forever -
For those capable of achieving it

I believe that the days pass and lose the brightness of their glory,
And in the end all that remains are the quiet whispers of one’s own life story

I believe that to be free is to be given the choice to become or to simply not to be

I believe that each moment should be lived
And experienced as if its actions echoed an eternity

I believe that in life the pursuit of certainty
Ends only in death,
And that this is the one forgotten Truth,
Corrupted by centuries of misinterpretations.

J. A. GUERRA OVERTON - "THE FOUNDER"
